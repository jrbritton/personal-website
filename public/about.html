About Me

Introduction

Hello! My name is James. I was born and raised in the United Kingdom, and went to school in Yorkshire in the north of England. Since I was young, I have had a keen interest in languages as well as computers, which eventually led me to study linguistics as a mature student, and eventually learn more about programming and statistics as part of my studies. I have been living in Hong Kong for many years now after originally visiting for an exchange programme during my undergraduate degree, and I have recently completed my PhD.

These days, my main area of interest within linguistics is embodied cognition, which tries to explain the way that we understand the world and use language via our interaction with our surroundings. It involves work on sensory perception, conceptual metaphors, and image schematics. As well as embodied cognition, I also work on other studies related to psycholinguistics and language cognition.
